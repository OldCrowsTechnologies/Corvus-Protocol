# Corvus Protocol — Art Brief: The Pale Chorus + Environment

Use this with whatever tool/artist produced the Murder renders (corvus/sage/pip/mira/rosa) to keep everything
in one consistent visual family. Feed the "Style Bible" paragraph into every prompt, then swap the per-subject block.

## Style Bible (reverse-engineered from the existing character renders)
Matte-glossy obsidian/statue-like creature sculpt, fine individually-sculpted feather or scale detail,
thin glowing hairline crack-veins running through the material in an accent color, single dramatic rim
light from upper-left, deep contact shadow, materials read as "carved onyx + inlaid gold hardware"
(gold beak/talons/perch fittings). Pure seamless white background, centered, product-turntable
framing. Camera: front, back, 3/4-left, left-profile — four renders per subject, same lighting rig,
same crop/scale across all four so they drop into a game engine turnaround without re-grading.

## The Pale Chorus — enemy prompts

**Wisp (fodder)**
Small hovering wraith, roughly bird-skull-and-tattered-shroud silhouette, semi-translucent pale
blue-grey vapor body, no visible limbs, faint hairline cracks of cold white-blue light beneath the
"skin." Should read as fragile and quick. Same style bible, but material is translucent vapor/glass
rather than solid obsidian — let the white backdrop show through at the edges.

**Wailer (debuffer)**
Gaunt hunched wraith with an over-wide, permanently-open jaw, trailing ragged shroud tendrils instead
of legs. Sickly olive-green crack-glow. Slightly larger than Wisp, hunched posture, one arm raised as
if mid-shriek.

**Shrieker (fast swarmer)**
Small, insectile/bat-like wraith — leathery membrane wings, no face, just a glowing magenta-pink
crack-vein mouth-slit. Compact, aerodynamic silhouette; implies it moves in a diving swarm of three.

**Husk (tank)**
Bulky armored wraith — think a knight's hollow suit of ash-grey bone-plate armor with nothing visible
inside the visor slit but violet crack-glow. Slow, heavy stance, cracked stone/bone plating texture
instead of feather/scale.

**Whisper (boss, 2 phases)**
Large, elaborate wraith-lord — tattered king's-shroud silhouette with antler-like crown of bone,
faceless hood with a single blinding white glowing core where a face would be. Phase 1: white
crack-glow, calm vertical drape. Phase 2: crack-glow shifts to blood-red, shroud whipping as if in
wind, posture more aggressive/lunging. Render both phases as separate turnarounds.

## Environment concept — "The Hollow Roots"
Direction: Norse (rune-carved standing stones, a dead World-Tree), Alice's Underland (impossible
scale — oversized fungus and lattice/checkerboard motifs half-buried in rock), the Underdark
(bioluminescent fungus as the only light source, black water, cavern ceiling lost in darkness),
Castlevania (gothic bone-arches and crumbling masonry fused into the cave walls, like a castle that
sank into the earth generations ago).

Concept shots to commission (matte painting style, moody, single readable focal point each):
1. Establishing wide shot: a shattered gothic archway swallowed by cavern rock, roots of a colossal
   dead tree breaking through the vaulted ceiling, teal-purple bioluminescent fungus clusters lighting
   the scene from below.
2. The summoning circle itself: a sunken stone dais carved with raven-and-rune motifs, standing at the
   bottom of the cavern like an altar, ringed by oversized glowing mushrooms at impossible scale.
3. A path detail: enemy spawn tunnel — a crack in a rune-stone wall, black water dripping, faint pale
   light (a Wisp) visible deep inside.

Palette to hand the artist: cavern rock #0c1420 → #1a2332, bioluminescent teal #00C2C7 and violet
#6B4FA0 as the *only* light sources, warm gold #B8922A restricted to carved rune inlays/altar metal —
never ambient light.
